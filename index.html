Abstract

Conventional UAV photogrammetry pipelines rely on sparse feature matching and Multi-View Stereo algorithms for dense reconstruction, typically requiring high image overlap ratios along with substantial post-processing computational overhead. This work proposes an incremental dense 3D reconstruction method based on the visual geometry grounded transformer model, which enables end-to-end inference of dense point clouds and camera poses directly from UAV imagery, thereby circumventing the constraints of the traditional "sparse-to-dense" two-stage reconstruction paradigm. Leveraging the global perception capability of the Transformer architecture, the proposed method achieves robust reconstruction under reduced overlap ratios and sparser camera viewpoints, effectively lowering data acquisition costs. A sliding window mechanism is employed to process image sequences in successive batches, facilitating 3D reconstruction concurrent with image acquisition. To address the multi-batch point cloud fusion challenge in incremental processing, a confidence-guided fusion strategy is designed that utilizes pixel-level confidence weights to resolve conflicts in overlapping regions while ensuring geometric continuity. The modular architecture of the proposed method allows seamless plug-and-play integration of different Visual Geometry Transformer models without requiring architectural modifications. Furthermore, the framework integrates GPS metadata for automatic georeferencing, supports direct generation of DSM from dense point clouds, and exports reconstruction results in a COLMAP-compatible format for seamless application to neural rendering techniques such as 3DGS, thus providing a streamlined data pathway for high-quality novel view synthesis and scene visualization.

Key Features

  • 🚀 Incremental Processing: Frame-by-frame image processing with real-time reconstruction updates
  • 🧠 Multi-Model Support: Plug-and-play integration of VGGT, FastVGGT, and MapAnything models
  • 🔗 Confidence-based Fusion: Intelligent point cloud merging with pixel-level confidence weights
  • 🌍 Georeferencing Export: Automatic UTM zone detection with multi-coordinate system support
  • 👁️ Real-time Visualization: Viser-based 3D point cloud and camera pose visualization
  • 📊 3DGS Compatible: COLMAP-format output for 3D Gaussian Splatting training

Experimental Results

Three state-of-the-art Visual Geometry Transformer models (VGGT, FastVGGT, and MapAnything) are systematically evaluated on terrain and building datasets. For geometric consistency, VGGT achieves the best alignment accuracy on terrain data with RMS error of 16.61m under confidence-based merging, while FastVGGT performs best on building data with 4.07m. The confidence-based merging strategy consistently outperforms points-only merging by effectively suppressing noise from uncertain depth estimates. For novel view synthesis quality, VGGT-based dense reconstruction significantly outperforms traditional SfM sparse reconstruction when used as initialization for 3D Gaussian Splatting training, achieving substantially higher PSNR, SSIM, and lower LPIPS scores across both datasets without relying on densification post-processing.
